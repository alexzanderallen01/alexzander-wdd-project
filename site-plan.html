Overview

Purpose

The purpose for this website is to gather and share information on rafting. As this web site grow, so will everything that can be learned though this web site. This may start off slow, but the projects for this web site will grow in size and range of topics it covers. There is a lot to learn about rafting for those who are just starting and people who are already experienced can come find new challenges and goals here. This web site will be here to gather knowledge on rafting and eventually share it with others who want to gather knowledge on rafting themselves.

Audience

The target customers for this site are going to be those who are learning about rafting or want to take trip and try it along the way. They will most likely be between the ages of 18 years and 27 years old, with the exception of younger children learning with them. These people will most likely have a tight budget, but with a little play since they can take on a new hobby. Most of the people who view this site will be living in the US. This site will most likely be access from a laptop or desktop but could also be accessed from a phone. It is easier to do a lot of research from a computer, but some may quickly look at the site or need some tips while on the move, so it is likely many will use it on a phone.

Typography

Heading Font: EXO 2

I choose to use Exo 2 as my head font for its smooth look and for how large it is. I liked how it looked in all of the examples. If used right, I thing it will be perfect for this website.

Paragraph Font: Roboto

I choose to use Roboto as my paragraph font for how easy it is to read. It looks professional while still being friendly to read.

The Site Map of a site is just like it sounds…it is a map of the pages in a site and how they are related and linked together. From the map above we can see that we will eventually have the Home page and 2 sub or child pages.

The lines that connect them all together indicate that each page should be accessible from any other page, it is essentially showing us the global navigation for the site.

Wireframes

Wireframes are like blueprints for making webpages. They should show the major sections of content that will be on the page and the relative locations of each element. In the wireframe below you can see there will be 6 sections to our page:

  1. At the top we have a section with the logo (the box with the mountain means an image) and the navigation bar.
  2. Then there is a banner image that stretches all the way across the screen.
  3. Next we have some text and an image
  4. ...followed by another row made up of an image and some text.
  5. Then one more section of text with no image.
  6. Lastly, a footer containing a copyright/name line and 3 social media icons.
